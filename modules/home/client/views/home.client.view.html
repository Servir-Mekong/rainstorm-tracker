Rainstorms tracker

The rainstorm tracker relies on a peer-reviewed spatiotemporal storm recognition method called Spatiotemporal Object-based Rainfall Analysis method (ST-CORA). This method is developed by Laverde-Barajas et al.(2019) to analyse the main characteristics of the storm events at the catchment scale. With ST-CORA with Multivariate kernel density estimation (KDE) proposed Laverde-Barajas et al. (2020a) storm can be segmented using the four-dimensions of the storm. The 4D object-based storm recognition is composed into the following steps.

Spatiotemporal Continuous rainfall Analysis: A multidimensional connected-labelling component is implemented to identify convective rainfall systems in space and time.

Probabilistic storm segmentation (KDE): Storm events are segmentation using a multivariate edge detection based on Kernel Density Estimation

Multi Threshold analysis: This process is applied to identify substorm events over large convective systems.

Storm track detection: Definition of the storm track using the skeletal structure of the storm event.
